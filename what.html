What Are Neural Networks?

Overview and types of neural networks.

Overview

Neural networks are a subset of machine learning and are at the heart of deep learning algorithms. Mimicking the human brain, neural networks utilize neurons and nodes to process input into output.

The human brain has neurons and synapses that can activate and send signals to each other to process information. Neural networks are loosely modelled after this. In a neural network, there are layers of interconnected nodes that use algorithms and mathematical calculations to map input to output. Deep learning is known as a universal approximator due to this. They are also designed to recognize patterns and can adapt from their mistakes.

Types of Neural Networks

Feedforward neural networks have nodes that are connected to all nodes in the next layer, and information goes forward with feedback process.

Backpropagation uses corrective feedback loops to find one correct path through the neurons for an optimal solution.

Convolutional neural networks are just neural networks that have a few additional layers for image recognition and classification.
